---
title: "Activity Management"
description: "Learn about Ducky's Activity Management module and how to configure it."
---

This page provides the essential configuration options for Ducky's **Activity Management** module, which provides an easy way to manage and track your staff's activity with LOAs and activity waves. To enable and configure this module, use the **`/setup`** command and navigate to the **Activity Management** page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="LOAs Enabled" titleSize="h2">
    Enabling this option will allow your staff members to request a **Leave of Absence** via the **`/loa request`** command.
  </Step>
  <Step title="LOA Logs Channel" titleSize="h2">
    The **LOA Logs Channel** is where all LOA requests and logs will be sent. Any user with access to this channel can accept or deny LOA requests.
  </Step>
  <Step title="LOA Role" titleSize="h2">
    The **LOA Role** is assigned to staff members with an active LOA.
  </Step>
  <Step title="LOA Nickname" titleSize="h2">
    The **LOA Nickname **toggles whether or not staff members get a nickname once their LOA request has been accepted.

    You may use these variables within the **LOA Nickname**:

    - **`{member.name}`**: The name of the member on LOA.
    - **`{member.username}`**: The username of the member on LOA.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit Max LOA Length" titleSize="h2">
    The **Edit Max LOA Length** is a setting to edit the maximum time a staff member may be on an active LOA.\
    \
    The formatting for this is:

    - **1m - 1 minute**
    - **1h - 1 hour**
    - **1d - 1 day**
    - **30d - 30 days**

    <Warning>
      Using any different format will result in the max LOA Length **not working**.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit LOA Nickname" stepNumber={6} titleSize="h2">
    The **Edit LOA Nickname **allows your to edit what the staff members username will be **after** their LOA gets accepted.

    Examples:

    - **LOA | {member.name} - LOA | [Discord display name]**
    - **LOA | {member.username} - LOA | [Discord username]**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle Tracking Modcalls" titleSize="h2">
    The **Toggle Tracking Modcalls **is a toggle where you can enable/disable tracking modcalls the staff member has responded to.

    This could be used to monitor staff members answering modcalls on-duty.
  </Step>
</Steps>
